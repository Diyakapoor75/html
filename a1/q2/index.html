environment introduction

The environment refers to the complex of physical, chemical, and biotic factors that surround and influence the survival, growth, and development of living organisms. This broad concept encompasses everything from the natural world to human-made surroundings and social conditions.

Biotic Components: All living or once-living elements, including plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms.
Abiotic Components: The non-living physical and chemical factors that make up habitats, including the atmosphere (air), hydrosphere (water), lithosphere (land and soil), and solar energy.

Types of Environment

Natural Environment: The natural world comprising all ecosystems, biomes, and natural phenomena that exist without large-scale civilized human intervention (e.g., oceans, forests, deserts).Human-Made Environment: Areas and structures created or heavily modified by human activity, such as urban cities, agricultural lands, and built infrastructure.Social and Cultural Environment: The intangible circumstances, cultural influences, and societal factors that affect daily human life and psychological well-being.
